What this page does

Explainer included
What

Calculates base and adjusted UPS load, recommends a standard frame, compares VRLA and LTO assumptions, and provides planning-level feeder / OCPD / rigid conduit outputs.

Why

UPS sizing depends on kVA, kW, runtime energy, power factor, load behavior, and margin. This page keeps those drivers visible.

How

Each load row is converted into kVA and kW, then adjusted by demand, category factor, and spare margin. Battery sizing is then computed from real load, runtime, UPS efficiency, and chemistry assumptions.

Learn More

Expandable engineering context
Why UPS sizing is not just adding current

Current determines apparent power, but runtime comes from real power and stored energy. The inverter must carry kVA while the battery must sustain kW over time.

Why nonlinear loads need overhead

Electronic and rectifier-style loads can stress the UPS differently than smooth linear loads. The nonlinear factor is an engineering margin to reflect that behavior.

Why motor and inductive loads are treated separately

Inductive loads can bring reactive behavior, dynamic voltage sensitivity, and restart concerns. Even with steady-state current input, concept sizing often needs additional margin.

Why runtime depends on chemistry assumptions

Nominal battery energy is not the same as usable delivered energy. Usable fraction, aging allowance, reserve margin, and UPS efficiency all affect installed battery size.

Why conductor and conduit outputs are labeled preliminary

Final feeder sizing depends on the adopted code edition, ambient, conductor type, adjustment factors, terminations, grounding details, fault current, and manufacturer instructions. This page is for concept design and planning.
